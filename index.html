Covid-19, Cronyism, and Corruption: A Year of the Pandemic.

Dan Bunn, Emily Rix, Federica Dadone - May 2021

The government could have run the 2012 Olympics almost three times over, with the money they’ve handed out so far during the pandemic; a cool 24 billion pounds. Now's a good time then for them to follow their own guidelines on how to shop with taxpayers’ money: encouraging ‘free and open competition’ in order to achieve ‘value for money’.

Despite this, the vast majority of contracts were allocated through closed channels, including private WhatsApp conversations between business people and politicians. At times, high-value contracts went to companies specialised in pest control or jewellery rather than the supply of protective equipment.

As a former prime minister faces questions about lobbying the government on behalf of a failed finance firm during the pandemic, we look back at how the government spent your money and handled your data while the country was locked down.
